What is this?

The original Pee in the Pool was a live web application (2006). Audience members submitted comments during readings and performances, which were randomly inserted into the story text. During active events, the story would become almost unreadable under a flood of contributions. During quiet periods, the text was nearly clean.

This archive simulates that ebb and flow. By default, the number of visible comments follows a time-of-day cycle: busier in the evenings (when readings would happen) and quieter during the day. Use the slider to manually override the activity level, from “clean pool” to “reading night.”
